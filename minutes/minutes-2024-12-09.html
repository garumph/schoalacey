SCHOA BOARD MEETING MINUTES

DECEMBER 9, 2024

 

Present: Gordon Baldwin, Chick and Sandi Barcellona, John and Mindy Ewing, David Hamilton, Laurie Kirschner, Tracy MacMillan

 

The meeting was called to order at 7:04.

 

  • Sandi gave a report for the pipe committee. The county is bringing the remote video camera on Wednesday morning to repeat a scope of the drainage pipe. It is hoped that an assessment of the health of the pipe can be provided on the spot. We will be provided with a copy of the video. Jim Leamy from the county, who had been our contact, has retired, so we will now be working with his supervisor. Sandi reported that a liner for the pipe is not an option because it would collapse in the event of a full collapse of the current pipe. Our best options are a pipe-in-pipe fix, or an open cut to completely replace the pipe. Sandi has received two estimates for a pipe-in-pipe repair. Grants are being investigated to help with the cost of the eventual repair. Brian Muirhead is currently working on writing a grant. Tracy has submitted an official request to the county for all paperwork dealing with the development and building of the neighborhood. Results of all this activity, and the financial impact, will be discussed at the annual meeting in January.

 

  • Chick gave an update on the required Reserve Study Plan to provide for a financially healthy HOA as required by the State. CEDcore has been chosen to help us set up our Reserve Plan to fulfill state requirements and to provide for adequate finances to maintain the neighborhood infrastructure and cover recurring and emergency expenses. The pipe repair was not included in this initial tabulation. It is hoped the camera scope will show we have some time to build financing for this project, but there is no guarantee. An assessment will likely be required. It is further hoped that the assessment can be done in small increments, as in: annual dues plus a small assessment toward future pipe repair.

 

  • Tracy gave a report on the budget. There will not be enough income for the next year to cover expenses, so the board will be discussing an increase in annual dues at the annual meeting, as well as areas where we might cut some expenses. Landscaping is one area where we might be able to cut back on some services in order to save money. The results of the Reserve Study Plan will also help the Board determine how much dues will need to be increased. 

 

  • Laurie reported that our landscaper, Samuel, had called her to report branches and other detritus left along the fence line on Yelm by workers surveying for the future roundabout at Yelm and Spurgeon Creek, which will encroach on South Cove property. It is hoped the county will clean up their mess so we are not left with possibly having to pay Samuel to clean it up. The situation will be monitored.
